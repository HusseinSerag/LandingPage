Info about Egypt

The pyramids are ancient, iconic structures that have captured the imagination of people around the world for millennia. They are characterized by their distinctive triangular shape and were primarily built as monumental tombs for pharaohs and other high-ranking individuals in ancient Egypt.
The Nile River, the longest river in the world, flows through Egypt. It has played a vital role in the country's history, serving as a source of water, transportation, and fertile soil for agriculture.
Located on the west bank of the Nile near Luxor, the Valley of the Kings is a burial site for many Pharaohs of the New Kingdom period. It's famous for the discovery of King Tutankhamun's tomb in 1922.
The ancient Egyptians developed one of the earliest writing systems known as hieroglyphics. It is a system of pictorial symbols that was used for religious texts, monumental inscriptions, and more.
